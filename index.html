July's Featured Book

The Great Gatsby

by F. Scott Fitzgerald

Join us this July as we explore one of the greatest classics of American literature. Set in the summer of 1922, F. Scott Fitzgerald's third book stands as the supreme achievement of his career and a true classic of 20th-century literature.

The story of the mysteriously wealthy Jay Gatsby and his love for the beautiful Daisy Buchanan is an exquisitely crafted tale that has been essential reading since it was published.

Through our guided discussions and activities, we'll explore the novel's themes of decadence, idealism, social upheaval, and resistance to change.

Program Details

The Great Gatsby Reading Program

Beyond the Book Summer Book Club offers a structured, engaging approach to exploring "The Great Gatsby" by F. Scott Fitzgerald. This program is designed to enhance reading comprehension, critical analysis, and literary appreciation.

  • Program Dates: July 1st to July 31st, 2025
  • Session Options:
    • Tuesday/Thursday, 8:30-9:30 AM CEST
    • Monday/Wednesday, 7:00-8:00 PM CEST
  • Group Size: Maximum 5 students per group
  • Platform: Zoom meetings
  • English Level: Upper-Intermediate to Advanced
  • Book: "The Great Gatsby" by F. Scott Fitzgerald

Program Benefits

Enhanced Reading Skills

Develop advanced reading comprehension and analysis techniques through guided discussions and structured activities.

Interactive Discussions

Engage in meaningful conversations about themes, characters, and literary techniques in a small group setting.

Critical Thinking

Strengthen analytical skills by examining complex themes and making connections to real-world issues.

Writing Development

Improve writing abilities through reader's logs, reflections, and guided response activities.

Cultivate a Love for Reading

Develop a genuine passion for literature through engaging discussions and activities that make reading enjoyable and meaningful.

Extract Life Lessons

Learn how to identify valuable lessons from literature and apply them to your own life experiences and global contexts.

AI Literacy Skills

Discover how to effectively use AI tools to enhance your learning, research, and analysis of literature in the digital age.

Small Group Format

Benefit from personalized attention and equal participation opportunities in groups of maximum 5 students.

Cultural Context

Explore the historical and cultural context of "The Great Gatsby" and its relevance to contemporary society.

About the Tutor

Giulia Michieli

I am an IB DP student based in Spain with international experience, having lived in Hong Kong, the Netherlands, and Italy. I'm passionate about English Language and Literature, which I study at Higher Level in the IB program.

With experience as a math, biology, and English tutor both in-person and online, I started Beyond the Book to make a difference in how young readers engage with literature. My goal is to create an environment where students can develop their analytical skills while fostering a genuine love for reading.

Our Cause

Supporting Room to Read

Beyond the Book Summer Book Club is proud to partner with Room to Read, a global organization transforming the lives of millions of children through education. We believe in the power of literacy to change lives and create a more equitable world.

100% of profits from the Beyond the Book program will be donated to Room to Read to support their mission of ensuring children around the world have access to quality education and books.

By joining our program, you're not only enhancing your own reading journey but also contributing to literacy and educational opportunities for children worldwide.
